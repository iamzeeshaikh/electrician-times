<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">An oven that doesn't heat properly can be frustrating, especially when your food takes too long or comes out unevenly baked. A malfunctioning oven can be caused by various factors, ranging from faulty components to minor calibration issues. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In this guide, we'll explore the common reasons why your oven may not be reaching the right temperature, how to fix these problems, and the cost of replacing a faulty heating element. </span></p>
<h2><b>Why Is My Oven Not Heating Up to the Proper Temperature?</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Several issues could be responsible if your oven isn't reaching the correct temperature or heating. Here are the most common reasons that you might need to get </span><a href="https://ovenfixer.com.au/oven-repair/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">oven repair</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> job done: </span></p>
<h3><b>1. Faulty Heating Element (Electric Ovens)</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Electric ovens rely on two main heating elements: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>The bake element</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (located at the base of the oven) </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><b>The broil element</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (located at the top) </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If either of these elements is damaged or burnt out, your oven won't heat properly. A good way to check is to turn on the oven and observe the elements. They may need replacement if they don't glow red or show visible damage (blisters or cracks). </span></p>
<h2><b>2. Defective Igniter (Gas Ovens)</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Gas ovens use an igniter to light the burner. The oven won't heat properly if the igniter is weak or broken. Signs of a faulty igniter include: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The oven is taking too long to heat up </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">No glow from the igniter </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The oven is not lit at all </span></li>
</ul>
<h2><b>3. Malfunctioning Temperature Sensor or Thermostat</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The oven's temperature sensor measures the internal heat and signals the control board to adjust accordingly. If this sensor is malfunctioning, the oven may: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Overheat </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Underheat </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Not maintaining a steady temperature </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">To check if your sensor works correctly, measure its resistance using an ohmmeter. If the reading is inconsistent, the sensor should be replaced. </span></p>
<h2><b>4. Broken Oven Door Seal</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If the oven door doesn't seal properly, heat can escape, preventing the oven from reaching the correct temperature. Check the gasket around the oven door for cracks or wear, and go for</span> <a href="https://ovenfixer.com.au/oven-repair/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">oven repair services</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> if necessary. </span></p>
<h2><b>5. Issues with Power Supply</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For electric ovens, a tripped <a href="/understanding-circuit-breakers-types-and-functions/">circuit breaker</a> or loose wiring may prevent the oven from getting enough power to heat up. For gas ovens, ensure the gas supply is turned on and functioning correctly. </span></p>
<h2><b>6. Control Board or Relay Board Failure</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The </span><b>electronic control board</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> regulates the oven's heating cycles. If it malfunctions, the oven may fail to heat properly. This issue is less common but may require a professional repair. </span></p>
<h2><b>7. Clogged Gas Burner (Gas Ovens Only)</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A clogged burner can prevent the gas oven from heating correctly. Dirt, grease, or debris may block the burner holes, reducing gas flow. Regular cleaning can prevent this issue. </span></p>
<h3><b>How to Fix an Inaccurate Oven Temperature?</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now that we've identified the possible causes, let's go over how to fix an oven that isn't heating correctly. </span></p>
<h2><b>1. Check and Replace the Heating Elements</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you suspect a faulty heating element: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Turn off the oven and unplug it. </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Inspect the bake and broil elements for visible damage. </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">It likely needs replacement if the element is not glowing red when turned on. </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You can purchase a new heating element online or from an appliance repair store. Most replacement elements cost between $20 and $100. </span></p>
<h2><b>2. Test and Replace the Temperature Sensor</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">To check the temperature sensor: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Use a multimeter to measure its resistance. </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A properly functioning sensor should read 1,000 to 1,100 ohms at room temperature. </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">If the reading is inconsistent, replace the sensor. </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Depending on the brand, new temperature sensors cost around $30 to $80. </span></p>
<h2><b>3. Calibrate the Oven Thermostat</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Calibration may be needed if your oven is heating but not reaching the correct temperature. Here's how to do it: </span></p>
<ol>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Preheat the oven and place an oven thermometer inside. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Set the oven to 350°F (177°C) and wait 15–20 minutes. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Check the thermometer. You may need to adjust the thermostat if it reads higher or lower than the set temperature. </span></li>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">Refer to your user manual for instructions on calibration. </span></li>
</ol>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most ovens allow manual temperature adjustments through their settings. If calibration doesn't solve the issue, the thermostat may need replacement. </span></p>
<h2><b>4. Inspect and Replace the Oven Door Gasket</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If heat is escaping due to a faulty door seal: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Check the rubber gasket around the oven door. </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Look for cracks, looseness, or damage. </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">If needed, replace the gasket with a new one. </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">New oven door gaskets cost $20 to $50, and installation is simple. </span></p>
<h2><b>5. Reset the Power Supply</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For electric ovens, check if the circuit breaker has tripped. Reset it if necessary. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ensure the valve is turned on for gas ovens and the igniter works. </span></p>
<h2><b>6. Clean the Gas Burner (Gas Ovens Only)</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you suspect a clogged burner: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Turn off the gas supply. </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Remove the burner and clean the holes using a soft brush and warm, soapy water. </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Dry thoroughly before reattaching. </span></li>
</ul>
<h2><b>7. Replace the Igniter (Gas Ovens Only)</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A failing igniter is a common cause of heating problems in gas ovens. If it glows but doesn't ignite, it needs replacement. New igniters cost $30 to $100, and installation takes 30–60 minutes. </span></p>
<h2><b>8. Consult a Professional for Electrical or Control Board Issues</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> </span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If your oven still doesn't work after these steps, the control board or relay board may be faulty. Professional repairs can cost between $150 and $400, depending on the issue. </span></p>
