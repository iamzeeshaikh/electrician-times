<p>The rapid development of technology plays a cruel joke on us.&nbsp;Technologies have developed so much that their accumulation creates a feeling of helplessness in people.&nbsp;It is impossible to fully understand how this or that device works.&nbsp;An endless number of features, bugs, hidden settings and menu items makes you think about the finite resources of the human brain and human life.</p>

<p>On the one hand, smart machines and computers are created to make human life easier and better.&nbsp;On the other hand, a person often spends too much time and attention on all this diversity.</p>

<h2>Let's define the concepts</h2>

<p>Digitalization, artificial intelligence, bots, neural networks – we hear these words too often.&nbsp;So often that it’s time to figure it out and comprehensively evaluate whether it’s good or bad?</p>

<p>As usual, let's first define the terms.&nbsp;What characteristics can artificial intelligence have?</p>

<ul><li>Collection, accumulation and processing of information.</li>

<li>Self-study.</li>

<li>Distribution of information to the outside world.</li>

<li>Communication with a person.</li>

<li>Making decisions.&nbsp;Perhaps this is the most important point.</li>
</ul>

<p>These items may include systems such as a machines, robots, computers, production equipment, and so on.&nbsp;They all have some semblance of intelligence in one way or another.&nbsp;And have one or more of the characteristics described above.</p>

<p>But any artificial intelligence has one distinctive feature - it is created by man.&nbsp;This means that it was man who endowed him with certain abilities and parameters.</p>

<p>Well, have you figured out the terminology?&nbsp;Me not.&nbsp;The fact is that now the boundaries of various concepts are very blurred, and it is difficult to give a clear definition of one or another system.&nbsp;A typical example that is always at hand is a telephone.&nbsp;What is it - a computer, a self-learning device, a means of communication and entertainment, a means of controlling a person, artificial intelligence, a device for digitalization?</p>

<h2>A few words about digitalization</h2>

<p>The advantages of digitalization are obvious: it facilitates human labour in organizing and processing information.&nbsp;In our country, digitalization is often understood as the maximum transfer of human life to “digital tracks.”&nbsp;This includes payment cards, electronic documents, digital passports, biometrics, control of movement and access to facilities.</p>

<p>There are already volunteers who have had a chip implanted under their skin, which carries all the information about the owner - from his passport data and location to information about his health status and details for payment at the supermarket checkout.&nbsp;Comfortable?&nbsp;Maybe.</p>

<p>Digitalization works great as long as everything goes as it should.&nbsp;If you are a law-abiding citizen, a cog in the system, then you won’t even think about breaking or changing anything.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>Two quotes on topic:</p>

<p>“The best slave is the one who thinks he is free.”</p>

<p>“I've seen too much of the world.&nbsp;I'm a bad slave."</p>
</blockquote>

<p>But here it can be brought to the point that the line between a person and a robot will become almost intangible - both will obediently carry out the will of the “master”.&nbsp;There are more than enough films on this topic.</p>

<p>Perhaps the best example of artificial intelligence is neural networks.&nbsp;The main property of a neural network is the ability to quickly learn.&nbsp;A person only sets the desired vector at the initial stage, and the neural network continues on its own, exponentially increasing its knowledge and, dare I say it, creative abilities.</p>

<p>For example, a neural network creates texts that are almost indistinguishable from texts written by a person.&nbsp;An example is the YaLM (Yet another Language Model) language model from Yandex, on which Alice works.</p>

<p>There are other services, for example, for autocomplete sites.&nbsp;This unpleasant phenomenon is gaining momentum in RuNet.&nbsp;The neural network takes several articles on a given topic, and in a few seconds generates quite adequate text with high uniqueness.&nbsp;As a blogger who writes articles with his hands and head, this phenomenon is extremely unpleasant for me, but I don’t know how to deal with it.</p>

<h2>How neural networks are introduced into humans</h2>

<p>I have already talked about chips implanted under human skin (an invasive method).&nbsp;Here it is more correct to talk not about neural networks, but about neural interfaces.&nbsp;But what prevents the neural interfaces of many people from being combined into a single neural network?</p>

<p>Initially, such technologies were developed to significantly improve the lives of people with disabilities.&nbsp;For example, paralyzed people can control cybernetic prostheses, computers and other gadgets in this way.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" width="500" height="281" src="/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/An-example-of-an-invasive-neural-interface.webp" alt="" class="wp-image-1080"/></figure>

<p>Further more.&nbsp;Nowadays, many companies have mastered technology that allows them to write texts and communicate on social networks without using a keyboard, using, so to speak, the power of thought.&nbsp;Of course, this method of communication makes it possible to play computer games.</p>

<p>The most famous company in this area is Elon Musk's Neuralink.&nbsp;Her successes in the field of neural interface are amazing.&nbsp;Neuralink managed to move further than its competitors did.</p>

<p>Now a completely wireless chip implanted under the skin is being tested on monkeys.&nbsp;But I'm sure people are also participating in the trials.</p>

<p>The main thing is that Neuralink technology allows you to transmit information directly to the human brain.&nbsp;For people, installing such a chip makes it possible to restore vision.&nbsp;Even for those who have not had the opportunity to see since birth.&nbsp;After all, the visual part of the cerebral cortex is still functioning.&nbsp;The same applies to people with hearing loss.</p>

<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" width="500" height="333" src="/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/Neuralink-N1-chip-implanted-under-the-skin.webp" alt="" class="wp-image-1081"/></figure>

<p>In the future, a completely immobilized person will be able to find a full life.&nbsp;After all, information can be stimulated and transmitted to any part of the brain.&nbsp;And the artificial reality received in the brain can be augmented, embellished, changed.&nbsp;For a neural network there is freedom here!</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>An example of such a future is shown in the film “Surrogates”, when people lay at home, and instead of them humanoid android robots lived a full life.</p>

<p>Spoiler: the main villain who created these androids united them into a neural network and thus gained power over all of humanity.&nbsp;But then Bruce Willis, as usual, saved everyone and returned everything “back”.</p>

<h2>Soon it will be difficult to distinguish a person from a robot</h2>

<p>I hope I conveyed to the reader the main idea of the article - any artificial intelligence endowed with logical thinking and the ability to create and make decisions can be harmful and dangerous to humans.&nbsp;It is even more dangerous when such intelligence takes the form of a neural network capable of learning.</p>

<p>Criticizing?&nbsp;I offer!&nbsp;To ensure that the neural network does not pose a danger, it must be limited by the following rules:</p>

<ol><li>The operation of any system with artificial intelligence can be terminated at any time without harm to the person and the system.&nbsp;That is, in one form or another there should be an easily accessible “Emergency Stop” or “Cancel” button.&nbsp;For example, it is necessary that the robot can be turned off in any situation, and the use of the neural interface can be easily stopped.</li>

<li>A person should be able to immediately understand that they are dealing with a robot.&nbsp;That is, any artificial intelligence should immediately make it clear to a person what he is dealing with.&nbsp;As with the law on advertising - looking at any text or image, we must immediately understand that this is advertising.</li>
</ol>

<p><strong>This is perhaps the most important law, especially relevant today.&nbsp;I would even put it as the first law of robotics:</strong></p>

<p><strong>“The first thing a robot should do when meeting a human is to identify itself as a robot.”</strong></p>

<h2>The laws of robotics need to change</h2>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><p>The science fiction prophet of Russian-Belarusian origin Isaac Asimov proposed the famous three laws of robotics, which would be logical to mention in the context of this article.&nbsp;These are the laws in my interpretation:</p>

<p>1. The robot must take care of human safety.<br>2. A robot must obey human orders unless this contradicts the first law.<br>3. A robot must take care of its own safety, unless this contradicts the first and second laws.</p>

<p>In my opinion, these laws are extremely vague.&nbsp;Both the robot and the person can interpret them in any way they want, depending on their understanding of the situation and immediate benefit.&nbsp;Isaac Asimov himself repeatedly raises the topic of these contradictions in his stories.</p>

<p>Many films have been created on this topic, in which artificial intelligence makes decisions that are disastrous for humans or even for humanity as a whole.&nbsp;And the robot makes such a decision for a simple and logical reason - it begins to consider a person a dangerous creature.</p>

<p>The main thing is that humanity must apply the laws of robotics first of all to itself.&nbsp;Of course, this seems like a utopia, but if it happened, the world would definitely be a better place.</p>
</blockquote>
