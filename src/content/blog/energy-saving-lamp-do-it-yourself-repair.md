<h2 id="работа">How fluorescent lamps work: 4 phases of start-up and shutdown - a simple explanation</h2>

<p>Inside the sealed space of the glass flask there are mercury vapors that create an ultraviolet radiation spectrum.&nbsp;It is converted into visible light by a phosphor deposited on the inner surface of the tube.</p>

<p>The gas discharge that causes the glow flows between the electrodes formed by the filaments. To ignite it, a throttle and a starter are used.</p>

<h3 id="разборка">How to disassemble energy-saving lamps: tips for beginners</h3>

<p>The lamp body consists of two detachable parts. The gap between them is barely noticeable. It can be filled with sealant or without it. This can be determined with a thin, sharp blade. For example, a stationery knife.</p>

<h3 id="ЭПРА">Electronic ballast repair: what to look for</h3>

<p>The easiest way to check the serviceability of ballasts is to connect them to a bulb with intact filaments and apply an input voltage of 220. If the lamp lights up, then the electronic ballast is working. Otherwise, you need to look for faults.</p>

<p>Usually, the owner buys not one, but several identical lamps in the store for lighting purposes. When they fail, they should not be thrown away, but the cause of the failure should be checked.</p>

<p>Quite often you can assemble one serviceable one out of two damaged ones.&nbsp;There will still be spare parts that will also come into use over time.</p>

<h2 id="советы">Safety tips for repairing energy-saving lamps</h2>

<p>I assume that you have experience working under voltage, but pay attention to:</p>

<ul><li>use of an isolation transformer;</li>

<li>use tools only with dielectric handles;</li>

<li>eliminating cases of unstable body position;</li>

<li>the need to turn your face away from the equipment being tested when voltage is applied and to place the electronic ballast and flask just in case in some box with a lid.</li>
