<h2 id="ошибки">Electrical mistakes that cause fires</h2>

<p>Even inside buildings made of brick, concrete or<br>other non-combustible materials, people use highly flammable items:<br>fabrics, paper, wooden furniture, and insulation, which are capable of ignition from<br>an electric spark or current discharge.</p>

<p>The situation worsens sharply in houses made of wood, where fire can spread to the material from which the building itself is made. It can burn out in a very short time.</p>

<p>This cannot be allowed.&nbsp;Therefore, we will figure out how to<br>eliminate electrical errors that could cause a fire.</p>

<p>Among them, several categories can be roughly distinguished when:</p>

<ol><li>the cables and wires laid in the house<br>overheat from the applied load, which means they are selected or installed<br>incorrectly;</li>

<li>randomly occurring emergencies<br>are not eliminated promptly when protection is not established or is absent;</li>

<li>wire connections create an increased contact resistance of the electrical<br>contact, which causes increased heating;</li>

<li>the contacts of plugs and sockets are not provided<br>with normal compression of the springs and are weakened, overheating themselves and heating the surrounding<br>space.</li>
</ol>

<h3 id="выбор">How to choose a cable according to the load for household wiring without errors</h3>

<p>I have already described the basic rules for selecting and calculating cables in an article about electrical installation work in<br>an apartment.&nbsp;The home electrical project is done in the same way.</p>

<p>As a result of its creation, you should have a completely clear picture of the wiring arrangement, starting from the power input from the incoming power line or alternative energy sources to each consumer.</p>

<p>At first glance, they have double insulation and a flat shape,<br>which is convenient for laying inside household wiring.&nbsp;In addition, their cost is more attractive for people who want to save on everything.</p>

<p>However, pay attention to its electrical characteristics.<br>Manufacturers indicate the maximum voltage level for the insulation layer: 250 volts.&nbsp;It just makes its way higher.&nbsp;And such cases happen quite often.</p>

<p>When indicating the cross-section of the metal core, the manufacturer himself allows it to be reduced by up to 30%.&nbsp;This is explained by the fact that PUNP wire<br>is produced in violation of GOST requirements according to the technical conditions of the plant where it is produced.</p>

<p>As a result, with a small voltage surge, the insulation breaks through, and when the maximum load of 3 kilowatts is reached, which a cross-section of 2.5 mm sq copper should normally withstand, additional overheating is created, destroying the insulation.</p>

<p>Fire statistics show that half of the incidents in the household network are caused by the use of PUNP wire. Since<br>the market economy does not prohibit its sale, we should rely only on our knowledge.</p>

<p>When choosing a wire, I always measure the diameter of its current-carrying core and calculate its cross-sectional area. I introduced this rule a long time ago when I was still rewinding the windings of motors and transformers.</p>
