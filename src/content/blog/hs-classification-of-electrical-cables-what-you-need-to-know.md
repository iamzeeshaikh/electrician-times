<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cables come in various forms, each classified under different Harmonized System (HS) codes based on their specifications and intended use. Proper classification ensures that importers pay the correct duties and avoid delays or penalties.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Common Types of Electrical Cables and Their HS Codes:</span></h2>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Power Cables (HS 8544): For transmitting high-voltage electricity in industrial and residential installations.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Data and Communication Cables (HS 8544): Includes Ethernet, fiber-optic, and coaxial cables for data transmission.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Electric Vehicle Wiring (HS 8544): Specialized cables designed for high-voltage systems in electric vehicles.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Renewable Energy Cables (HS 8544): Used in solar and wind energy projects.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Example: Fiber-optic cables are classified based on their core material and function, which affects applicable tariffs and regulatory requirements. Correct classification is critical to avoid misdeclarations that can lead to penalties.</span></p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Rules of Origin for Cables: Ensuring Compliance with Trade Agreements</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For manufactured goods like cables, determining the correct rules of origin is essential to benefit from preferential tariffs under trade agreements such as USMCA or UK-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement (TCA).</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Common Origin Rules for Cables:</span></h3>
<ol>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Change in Tariff Classification (CTC): The finished cable must have a different HS code than its raw materials.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;" aria-level="1"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Regional Value Content (RVC): A minimum percentage of the product’s value must originate from eligible countries.</span></li>
</ol>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Example: A cable produced in Mexico using imported copper may qualify for preferential treatment under USMCA if it meets the CTC or RVC requirements.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Anti-dumping duties (ADDs) are frequently imposed on electrical items, including cables, to protect domestic industries. Businesses must stay informed about these measures to avoid unexpected costs.</span></p>
